**The Good Stuff**



* Double-check before you hit "Share": We’ve all seen those weird rumors fly around. Take ten seconds to make sure a post is actually true so you don't accidentally spread fake news.



* Set a "Scroll Timer": It’s easy to lose three hours to Reels when you should be studying or sleeping. Use an app timer—future you (especially during exams) will thank you.



* Be the person who adds value: If you’re going to comment, make it thoughtful. Building real connections with peers online can actually lead to some cool opportunities later.



* Lock down your settings: Every now and then, check who can actually see your location and contacts. Keeping your profile private isn't being "antisocial"—it's being smart.



* Remember to look up: Don't forget there's a world outside the screen. Take regular breaks to recharge and hang out in the real world.



**The "Watch Out" Stuff**



* Don't overshare the "Blueprint": Keep your exact location, daily schedule, and any financial info off your stories. Some things are better kept offline.



* Stay away from the drama: Cyberbullying or trolling isn't worth the energy. If you wouldn't say it to someone's face, it probably shouldn't be a comment.



* Mute the "Comparison Trap": If a certain account makes you feel like your life isn't "perfect" enough, just unfollow. FOMO is real, but it doesn't have to run your life.



* Skip the "Sus" links: If a link looks weird or an app asks for way too many permissions, it’s a trap. Don't risk a malware headache for a random click.



* The 5-Second Pause: Before you post something impulsive, take a breath. If it's something you’ll regret in a job interview three years from now, keep it in the drafts.
