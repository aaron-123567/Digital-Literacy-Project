**The "Do" List**



* Fact-check before you hit share: We’ve all seen fake news go viral. Take ten seconds to make sure it’s legit so you aren’t "that guy" spreading rumors.



* Set a scroll limit: It’s easy to lose three hours to Reels. Set an app timer so your sleep and assignments don't suffer.



* Be the "Good Vibes" person: If you’re going to comment, make it count. Thoughtful interactions actually help you build a network; mindless lurking doesn't.



* Lock down your tech: Go through your settings once a month. Make sure only the people you actually know can see where you are or what you're up to.

S

* Log off and look up: Real life happens offline. Take regular breaks to recharge—your brain (and your eyes) will thank you.



**The "Don’t" List**



* Don’t give away the blueprint: Avoid posting your exact location, daily schedule, or anything financial. Keep a little mystery for safety's sake.



* Don’t feed the trolls: Cyberbullying isn't just "mean"—it’s exhausting. If you wouldn't say it to someone's face, don't type it behind a screen.



* Stop the "Comparison Trap": If a certain account makes you feel like your life isn't "aesthetic" enough, unfollow them. FOMO is a choice!



* Avoid the "Sus" links: If a link looks weird or an app asks for too many permissions, skip it. It’s not worth the malware headache.



* The 5-Second Rule: Before you post something impulsive, take five seconds to breathe. If it’s something you might regret during a job interview in three years, keep it in the drafts.
